Seven Warring States — c. 260 BCE
The map shows the territorial extent of the seven major states before Qin's final conquests. Chu (楚) dominated the south with its capital at Ying (郢), controlling the Yangtze River basin and the lakes of modern Hubei and Hunan.
Key States
Chu 楚 — Southern
Qin 秦 — Western
Qi 齊 — Eastern
Note: Qu Yuan died in 278 BCE, shortly after Qin captured Ying.
