Warring States
屈原 Qu Yuan c. 340–278 BCE

Works

離騷 Li Sao · 九歌 Nine Songs · 天問 Heavenly Questions

Life

Minister of Chu, slandered and exiled. Drowned himself in the Miluo River after Chu fell to Qin.

Legacy

Father of Chinese poetry. Template for the wronged loyal minister.
宋玉 Song Yu c. 290–223 BCE

Works

九辯 Nine Debates · 高唐賦 Gaotang Fu · 神女賦 Goddess Fu

Life

Disciple of Qu Yuan. Known for beauty, eloquence, and sensuous dream-encounter imagery.

Legacy

Bridge between Chuci and Han fu. Inventor of the goddess-encounter narrative.
Han Dynasty
賈誼 Jia Yi 200–168 BCE

Works

吊屈原賦 Lament for Qu Yuan · 鵩鳥賦 The Owl · 過秦論 Faults of Qin

Life

Brilliant young official, exiled due to jealousy at court. Later made tutor to Prince Liu Yi, who died in a horse-riding accident. Jia Yi blamed himself and died of grief a year later, at 33.

Legacy

Model of tragic genius cut down by politics. Established literary homage tradition.
司馬相如 Sima Xiangru 179–117 BCE

Works

子虛賦 Sir Fantasy · 上林賦 Shanglin Fu · 大人賦 The Great Man

Life

Master of grand fu. Eloped with widow Zhuo Wenjun. Court poet to Emperor Wu. Known for stuttering.

Legacy

Supreme Han fu stylist. Shanglin Fu set standard for imperial spectacle.
楊惲 Yang Yiin (Yang Yun) d. 54 BCE

Works

報孫會宗書 Letter to Sun Huizong

Life

Han official and writer whose self-defensive letter became a classic of political prose and personal voice under pressure.

Legacy

Friday pp. 157-169 opening text: rhetorical self-fashioning, grievance, and literati ethics.
Six Dynasties
嵇康 Ji Kang (Hsi K'ang in Birch) 223–262 CE

Works

與山巨源絕交書 Letter to Shan Tao

Life

Philosopher, musician, and member of the Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove; executed after political conflict at court.

Legacy

Friday pp. 157-169: paradigmatic refusal of office and the ethics of withdrawal.
陶潛 / 陶淵明 Tao Yuanming (T'ao Ch'ien in Birch) 365–427 CE

Works

桃花源記 Peach Blossom Spring

Life

Civil official who repeatedly left office and returned to farming life. Canonical poet of withdrawal and rustic integrity.

Legacy

Core voice for Friday's pages 157-169 recluse-poetry unit: retreat as an ethical and aesthetic choice, not simple escapism.
孔稚珪 Kong Zhigui (Kung Chih-kuei in Birch) 447–501 CE

Works

北山移文 Proclamation on North Mountain

Life

Southern dynasties writer known for satirical prose criticizing opportunistic officials who fake reclusion for prestige.

Legacy

Friday pp. 157-169 closing text: exposes the politics of "performing" withdrawal.
