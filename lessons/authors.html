文人

Literary Authors from Birch's Anthology

屈原 Qu Yuan c. 340–278 BCE
Warring States · Chu
離騷 Li Sao · 九歌 Nine Songs · 天問 Heavenly Questions
Minister of Chu, slandered and exiled. Drowned himself after Chu's fall to Qin.
Father of Chinese poetry. Template for the wronged loyal minister.
宋玉 Song Yu c. 290–223 BCE
Warring States · Chu
九辯 Nine Debates · 高唐賦 Gaotang Fu · 神女賦 Goddess Fu
Disciple of Qu Yuan. Known for beauty, eloquence, and sensuous imagery.
Bridge between Chuci and Han fu. Dream-encounter narratives.
賈誼 Jia Yi 200–168 BCE
Early Han
吊屈原賦 Lament for Qu Yuan · 鵩鳥賦 The Owl · 過秦論 Faults of Qin
Brilliant young official, exiled due to jealousy. Died young of grief.
Model of tragic genius. Established literary homage tradition.
司馬相如 Sima Xiangru 179–117 BCE
Western Han
子虛賦 Sir Fantasy · 上林賦 Shanglin Fu · 大人賦 The Great Man
Master of grand fu. Eloped with widow Zhuo Wenjun. Court poet to Emperor Wu.
Supreme Han fu stylist. Shanglin Fu set standard for imperial spectacle.
枚
枚乘 Mei Sheng d. 140 BCE
Western Han
七發 Qi Fa (Seven Stimuli)
Served several princes. Created new form where visitor cures prince through vivid descriptions.
Invented the "Seven" genre. Pioneer of escalating rhetorical set-pieces.
班固 Ban Gu 32–92 CE
Eastern Han
兩都賦 Two Capitals Fu · 漢書 History of Han
Historian and poet. Completed Han Shu. Died in prison after political implication.
Redirected fu toward moral purpose. Template for capital-city fu.
曹操 Cao Cao 155–220 CE
Late Han · Wei
短歌行 Short Song · 觀滄海 Viewing the Ocean
Warlord who unified the north. Genuine poet who gathered literary talents at court.
Pioneer of Jian'an style—direct, vigorous, unadorned.
曹植 Cao Zhi 192–232 CE
Wei
洛神賦 Goddess of the Luo · 白馬篇 White Horse · 七步詩 Seven-Step Poem
Most gifted of Cao Cao's sons. Lost succession, lived under suspicion.
Greatest Jian'an poet. Model of talent thwarted by politics.
陶潛 Tao Qian 365–427 CE
Eastern Jin
歸去來辭 Return · 桃花源記 Peach Blossom Spring · 飲酒 Drinking Wine
Resigned office rather than bow. Farmed, drank, wrote in poverty.
Patron saint of reclusion. Model for "fields and gardens" poetry.
謝靈運 Xie Lingyun 385–433 CE
Liu Song
山水詩 Landscape poetry · 登池上樓 Ascending the Tower
Arrogant aristocrat. Famous for hiking boots and mountain expeditions. Executed for rebellion.
Father of landscape poetry. Made scenery the subject, not backdrop.
