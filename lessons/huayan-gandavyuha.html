Teaching Focus

Core Claims for Class

Big Numbers as Technique

The text repeatedly scales up to dust-particles, world-systems, and cosmic ages. Treat this as a literary device that produces awe and stretches ethical imagination.

Infinity Vocabulary

Words like “infinite,” “countless,” and “inconceivable” are part of Huayan poetics. They signal that awakening exceeds ordinary narrative proportion.

Many Teachers

In the Gandavyuha account, Sudhana learns from fifty-three spiritual friends, including laywomen and female spirits. Authority is distributed, not monopolized.

Social Disruption

The chapter challenges purity hierarchies: laypersons and women are full teachers, not secondary participants. This is central to classroom discussion.

Class Plan

20-Minute Flow

0-4 min

Set the stakes

Prompt: “When a text says ‘countless’ instead of one number, what effect does it create?” Gather 3 responses.

4-10 min

Read evidence aloud

Read the three source cards below in order: (1) scale, (2) Sudhana’s teacher network, (3) equality of lay and women teachers.

10-16 min

Pair interpretation

Pairs choose one excerpt and explain how it works as literary form, not just doctrine.

16-20 min

Synthesis + exit ticket

Exit ticket: “How do scale and social diversity work together in Huayan’s vision of awakening?”

Evidence Packet

Verified Excerpts for Class Use

A. Infinity and Scale
“...the plurality of spiritual paths... and the infinite opportunity... for the manifestation of... enlightenment.”
“...manifestation of countless bodies, as many as all the atomic particles in the plane of dharma...”

Source: Gomez, “Avatamsaka Sutra,” in Buddhist Spirituality (1993), pp. 162-163.

B. Sudhana's Many Teachers
“One by one, each of fifty-two teachers refers Sudhana to another spiritual friend.”
“...the fifty-third teacher... Samantabhadra...”

Source: Gomez, “Avatamsaka Sutra,” p. 164.

C. Inclusion Beyond Monastic Hierarchy
“...his teachers... include laymen, laywomen, Brahmins, and female nightspirits.”
“The women among Sudhana’s teachers do not have to become men...”
“...lay persons do not have to become ascetics...”

Source: Gomez, “Avatamsaka Sutra,” pp. 164-165.

Teaching note: Vasumitra/courtesan discussion belongs in this inclusion frame when reading Chapter 39 traditions across translations.

Discussion

Questions to Drive Seminar

Literary Form

  • What does numerical excess do that normal prose cannot do?
  • How does the text's scale change your sense of narrative realism?
  • Is “inconceivable” a limit, or a method?

Social Vision

  • Why present enlightenment through many social roles?
  • How does Sudhana's itinerary challenge gatekeeping?
  • Why include socially marginal figures as teachers?
After Class

Mini Assignment (5 minutes)

Write 4-5 sentences: Choose one excerpt above and explain how it connects form (big numbers, infinity language, repeated teacher encounters) with ethics (who can teach, who can awaken).
