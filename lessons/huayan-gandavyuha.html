華嚴經・入法界品 Huayan: Entry into the Dharma Realm Tang Buddhism
20-minute teaching module
Focus text: Gaṇḍavyūha / 入法界品 (last chapter of the Avataṃsaka/Huayan Sūtra). Theme: immense numbers, infinity language, and Sudhana's many teachers across social boundaries.
Goals 20-Minute Plan 3 Passages Discussion Sources
Lesson Framing

What Students Should Notice

1. Big Numbers

Huge counts and cosmic scales are not statistics. They are literary pressure that stretches readers' sense of reality and moral responsibility.

2. Infinity Language

“Innumerable,” “boundless,” and “inconceivable” build a poetics of non-closure. The text refuses a small, finite frame of awakening.

3. Many Teachers

Sudhana learns from monks, laywomen, rulers, children, artisans, and the courtesan Vasumitrā. The chapter attacks spiritual gatekeeping.

Class Flow

20-Minute Teaching Plan

0-3 min

Warm-up Question

Ask: “What happens to meaning when a text keeps saying ‘countless’ instead of giving one clear number?” Collect 3 student responses before introducing the chapter context.

3-9 min

Close Reading 1 + 2

  • Read Passage A (big numbers) aloud once.
  • Read Passage B (many teachers) and ask who counts as a “legitimate” teacher.
  • Have students underline one repeated phrase in each passage.
9-15 min

Pair Activity

In pairs, students choose one line and paraphrase it in plain modern English without losing its force. Share two examples and compare what gets flattened or lost.

15-20 min

Whole-Class Synthesis

  • Return to the courtesan Vasumitrā episode.
  • Ask what social assumptions the text disrupts.
  • Exit ticket: one sentence on how “infinity” changes ethics in Huayan.
Teaching Texts

Three Short Passages (Classroom Renderings)

Passage A · Vast Numbers
“In one instant, he beheld worlds as numerous as atoms in immeasurable Buddha-fields; in each world were immeasurable Buddhas, each teaching immeasurable beings across immeasurable kalpas.”
Teach it as: numerical excess as literary method. The piling-up creates scale, awe, and ethical expansion.
Passage B · Many Good Friends
“Sudhana went from teacher to teacher seeking the bodhisattva path: monastics and householders, rulers and servants, learned elders and ordinary workers, each opening one gate of liberation.”
Teach it as: distributed authority. No single social class controls access to truth.
Passage C · Vasumitrā
“He approached Vasumitrā, famed as a courtesan. Through her, he learned a liberating practice that transforms desire without clinging, compassion without exclusion.”
Teach it as: deliberate shock to purity norms. Huayan reframes who can embody wisdom.
Class Questions

Discussion Prompts

Interpretive

  • Why might the chapter repeat “immeasurable” so aggressively?
  • How do very large numbers function differently in religion than in science?
  • What kind of reader does this language try to produce?

Social & Ethical

  • Why include a courtesan among Sudhana's teachers?
  • Does this chapter flatten hierarchy, or rework it in a new way?
  • Where do you see this text resisting exclusion?
Instructor Notes

Quick Scholarly Anchor

For historical context on Chinese exegesis of the Avataṃsaka/Huayan corpus, use Imre Hamar, The Exegetical Tradition of the Buddhāvataṃsaka-sūtra (2022): open PDF. The three passages above are compact classroom renderings designed for discussion rather than strict philological translation.
