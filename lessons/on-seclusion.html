隱逸 The Problem of Withdrawal
概述 Overview

When does a scholar leave public life? These four texts—spanning four centuries—offer competing answers, each engaging differently with Confucianism and Daoism. "Seclusion" (yǐnyì 隱逸) is never merely personal: it is always political, philosophical, and a claim about what makes life meaningful.

四篇 The Four Texts
報孫會宗書 Letter to Sun Huizong W. Han · 1st c. BCE
Confucian Political Ethics

Author: Yang Yun 楊惲 · Trans. Burton Watson

Context

Yang Yun, grandson of Sima Qian, fell from court favor and retired to farming. His friend criticized him for enjoying life rather than performing shame.

The Argument

Yang deploys Dong Zhongshu's distinction: statesmen pursue benevolence; commoners pursue livelihood. Since Yang is now a commoner, Sun's standards don't apply.

"Those who do not follow the same road cannot lay plans for each other." — Confucius, Analects 15.40

Key Move

A Confucian defense of withdrawal: he accepts the framework but exits its jurisdiction by changing his social category.

與山巨源絕交書 Letter to Shan Tao Wei-Jin · 3rd c. CE
Daoist Anti-Confucian

Author: Xi Kang 嵇康 · Trans. J.R. Hightower

Context

One of the Seven Sages of the Bamboo Grove. When offered a government post, he wrote this refusal—ending a friendship. Later executed for nonconformity.

The Argument

"Laozi and Zhuangzi are my masters." He attacks Confucian culture-heroes (Tang, Wu Wang, Duke of Zhou) as regime propaganda.

Seven Things He Cannot Stand

  • Lying abed while heralds wait
  • Bowing in formal clothes while infested with lice
  • Funerals, paperwork, vulgar colleagues
  • Having his mind bound with worldly cares
"I am like the wild deer... dress it up with a golden bridle and it will but long the more for its native woods." — p. 163

Key Move

Wuwei (無為) as philosophical program: his bodily nature and his ideas are one.

桃花源記 Peach Blossom Spring E. Jin · c. 421 CE
Daoist Primitivism Utopian Critique

Author: Tao Qian 陶潛 · Trans. Cyril Birch

The Story

A fisherman stumbles into a hidden valley. Its people fled the Qin tyranny centuries ago; they don't know the Han existed. He leaves, tells authorities, and can never find it again.

Philosophical Echoes

Echoes Daodejing ch. 80: "Let there be a small state with few people... they will grow old and die without traveling."

"Since that time there have been no further 'seekers of the ford.'" — alludes to Analects 18.6

Why Unreachable?

  • Betrayal destroys utopia
  • Seeking it deliberately makes it vanish
  • The state destroys what it cannot control

Key Move

Utopia exists but is unrepeatable—a critique of the entire political order.

北山移文 Proclamation on North Mountain S. Qi · late 5th c. CE
Authentic vs. Fake Satire

Author: Kong Zhigui 孔稚珪 · Trans. J.R. Hightower

The Satire

Mountain spirits denounce Zhou Yong, who posed as a recluse but immediately accepted office when summoned. The mountains bar his return.

The Accusation

He "imitated Yan He's retirement, copied Nanguo's meditation"—figures from the Zhuangzi. He performed reclusion for career advancement.

"Wind-driven clouds grieved... Rock-rimmed springs sobbed... Grass and trees seemed to have suffered loss." — p. 171

What It Reveals

By the 5th century, "reclusion" was a recognized career path. Emperors sought famous hermits to demonstrate virtue. Being summoned was prestigious.

Key Move

Defends authentic withdrawal by satirizing fakers—assumes the Xi Kang position and uses it to condemn poseurs.

討論 Discussion Questions
Questions 1–3
  1. Can you opt out of Confucianism? Yang Yun claims he's now a commoner. Is this legitimate, or a rhetorical dodge?
  2. Why mix lice with philosophy? Xi Kang lists bodily discomforts alongside ethical objections. What does this say about his concept of "nature" (xing 性)?
  3. Why can't the fisherman return? Betrayal? The Daoist paradox of seeking? The state's destructive reach?
Questions 4–5
  1. How do you spot a fake recluse? Kong Zhigui assumes we can distinguish authentic from performative withdrawal. What are the criteria?
  2. Compare the four relationships to Confucianism: Yang uses it, Xi Kang rejects it, Tao Qian alludes ironically, Kong satirizes its misuse. Is there a coherent philosophy of seclusion?
